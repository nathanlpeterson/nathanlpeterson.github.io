This is a Power BI demo - Input is 3 columns for each second: Case (Device), TimeStamp, On/Off.
Attributes:
Device: Type of device used.
Case: A particular instance of a device - used by one particular patient. A Case is a combination of patient / device.
Event: An occurence of a particular device being On or Off.
Patient: Person who uses device.
Measures:
% Time On: Number of Seconds the device(s) were on divided by the Total Number of Seconds within context.
Avg Seconds Per Event: Number of Seconds the device(s) were on divided by the Number of Times the devices were switched on.
Number of Times On: Count of times devices were switched on.
Total Seconds On: Number of Seconds the device(s) were on.
Getting Around:
The lower left-hand corner of each page contains a Bookmark and Filter button. When clicked, these reveal their associated ribbon. Click the Left button to hide the ribbon.
Notes on the Sample Data:
Populated a row for each second of a single 24 hour period for each of 10 devices, with randomized On/Off. One drawback is that the Measures are Highly Correlated.
Because of this, it is difficult to demonstrate visualizing how multiple variables work with each other.
